Section 4 / Not in Any Sell-Side Model

Three Unmodeled Upsides

These are not in the probability-weighted return. Each is optionality. They either materialize or they do not. The base case target of $36 assumes none of them.

UPSIDE 01
AIBA Tariff Rebate Optionality
The U.S. Court of International Trade ruled that $160 billion in AIBA tariffs collected by the government must be rebated to importers of record. Insteel was not the importer of record on offshore wire rod purchases. Its suppliers collected those tariffs and now face a legal obligation to rebate them. CEO Woltz disclosed on the Q2 2026 earnings call that Insteel paid those tariffs through higher wire rod prices. Any supply chain pass-through is unmodeled EPS upside requiring no management action.
● Monitoring
Source: IIIN Q2 FY2026 Earnings Call Transcript, April 16, 2026 — CEO H.O. Woltz III
UPSIDE 02
IIJA Expiration Surge
$131.2 billion in IIJA funds become available for obligation in FY2026, per U.S. GAO data. The law expires September 30, 2026, which is also the end of Insteel's fiscal year. Federal agencies face a use-it-or-lose-it deadline. Projects queued for 18 months due to permitting delays will accelerate toward obligation in H2 2026. Every federally funded bridge, culvert, and drainage project requires Buy America-compliant domestic wire reinforcement. Insteel is the largest domestic manufacturer.
● Active — Law expires Sept 30, 2026
Source: U.S. GAO Report GAO-25-107243, April 2025 — U.S. DOT IIJA Funding Status, February 28, 2026
UPSIDE 03
Section 232 Full Customs Value Restructuring
The April 2, 2026 proclamation restructured Section 232 tariffs to apply to the full customs value of covered steel derivatives, effective April 6. PC strand is a covered derivative and Insteel's highest-margin product. Under the prior system, the 50% tariff applied only to metal content value, roughly 60 to 70% of the invoice. Under the new system, the tariff applies to the entire customs value. The effective import tariff burden on competing PC strand imports increased approximately 70 to 80%. This is not in the Sidoti model cut on April 17. It is not in the Zacks Strong Sell issued April 22.
● Effective April 6, 2026
Source: Federal Register, April 6, 2026 — Presidential Proclamation on Section 232 Tariff Restructuring. Confirmed by BDO, Perkins Coie, and GHY trade compliance analysis.

Section 6

Competitive Protection Tracker

Two government actions underpin the competitive moat analysis. Monitor whether they remain intact.

U.S. Department of Commerce
Final Affirmative Circumvention Determination — Welded Wire Mesh from Mexico
March 25, 2026  ·  Federal Register Doc. 2026-05809
Intact No legal challenge filed
Final affirmative circumvention determination. Imports of low-carbon steel wire produced in Mexico and assembled into standard welded wire mesh in the United States were found to be circumventing the antidumping and countervailing duty orders on welded wire mesh from Mexico. The loophole used since 2021 is permanently closed. Foreign competitors can no longer use U.S. finishing operations to bypass the AD/CVD orders on finished welded wire mesh products. This is Insteel's second largest product by revenue.
Source: U.S. International Trade Administration, Federal Register — March 25, 2026 (FR Doc. 2026-05809)
Presidential Proclamation
Section 232 Tariff Restructuring — Full Customs Value Applied to Steel Derivatives
Effective April 6, 2026  ·  Proclamation of April 2, 2026
Intact No reversal announced
Section 232 tariffs now apply to the full customs value of covered steel articles and specified derivative products, replacing the prior metal-content-value calculation method. Prestressed concrete strand is a covered derivative. The effective import tariff burden on competing PC strand imports increased approximately 70 to 80% versus the prior regime. Annex III provides a transitional reduced rate for certain categories through December 31, 2027. This restructuring occurred 24 days before the Q2 earnings miss and is not reflected in any published analyst estimate revision.
Source: Federal Register — April 6, 2026 Proclamation. Confirmed by BDO Trade Alert, Perkins Coie Update, and GHY Customs Corner (April 2026)
