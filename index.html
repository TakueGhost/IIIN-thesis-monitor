Section 4 — Not in Any Sell-Side Model

Three Unmodeled Upsides

These are not in the +28% probability-weighted return. Each is optionality that either materializes or it does not. The $36 base case target assumes none of them.

Upside 01
AIBA Tariff Rebate Optionality
The U.S. Court of International Trade ruled that $160 billion in AIBA tariffs must be rebated to importers of record. Insteel was not the importer of record on its offshore wire rod purchases. Its suppliers collected those tariffs and now face a legal obligation to rebate them. CEO Woltz disclosed on the Q2 2026 call that Insteel paid those tariffs through elevated wire rod prices. Any supply chain pass-through is unmodeled EPS upside requiring no management action.
Monitoring
Source: IIIN Q2 FY2026 Earnings Call, April 16 2026 — CEO H.O. Woltz III
Upside 02
IIJA Expiration Surge
$131.2 billion in IIJA funds become available for obligation in FY2026, per U.S. GAO data. The law expires September 30, 2026 — the end of Insteel's fiscal year. Federal agencies face a use-it-or-lose-it deadline. Projects queued for 18 months due to permitting delays will accelerate toward obligation in H2 2026. Every federally funded bridge and drainage project requires Buy America-compliant domestic wire reinforcement. Insteel is the largest domestic manufacturer.
Active — Law expires Sept 30
Source: U.S. GAO Report GAO-25-107243, April 2025 · U.S. DOT IIJA Status, Feb 28, 2026
Upside 03
Section 232 Full Customs Value Restructuring
The April 2, 2026 proclamation restructured Section 232 tariffs to apply to the full customs value of covered steel derivatives, effective April 6. PC strand is a covered derivative and Insteel's highest-margin product. Under the prior system the 50% tariff applied to metal content value only — roughly 60–70% of the invoice. Under the new system it applies to the entire customs value. The effective import tariff burden on competing PC strand imports increased approximately 70–80%. Not in the Sidoti model. Not in the Zacks downgrade.
Effective April 6, 2026
Source: Federal Register, April 6 2026 Proclamation · Confirmed by BDO, Perkins Coie, GHY trade analysis

Section 6 — Moat Integrity

Competitive Protection Tracker

Two government actions underpin the competitive moat. Monitor whether they remain intact.

Protection 01 — U.S. Dept. of Commerce
Final Affirmative Circumvention Determination — Welded Wire Mesh from Mexico
March 25, 2026 · Federal Register Doc. 2026-05809
Intact No legal challenge filed
Commerce found that LCS wire produced in Mexico and assembled into standard welded wire mesh on U.S. soil was circumventing the AD/CVD orders on welded wire mesh from Mexico. The loophole that let foreign product bypass duties for two years is permanently closed. This is Insteel's second-largest product by revenue.
Source: U.S. International Trade Administration, Federal Register — FR Doc. 2026-05809, March 25, 2026
Protection 02 — Presidential Proclamation
Section 232 Restructuring — Full Customs Value Applied to Steel Derivatives
Effective April 6, 2026 · Proclamation of April 2, 2026
Intact No reversal announced
Section 232 tariffs now apply to the full customs value of covered steel derivatives. PC strand is a covered derivative. The effective import tariff burden on competing PC strand imports increased approximately 70–80% versus the prior metal-content-only regime. This occurred 24 days before the Q2 miss and is not in any post-miss analyst model.
Source: Federal Register, April 6, 2026 Proclamation · Confirmed: BDO Trade Alert, Perkins Coie Update, GHY Customs Corner (April 2026)
